Lessons from ALU

Self-driven learning is the real curriculum

ALU doesn't hand you a syllabus and wait — it hands you a problem. Learning Docker, Linux server administration and firewall configuration by breaking and fixing labs taught me more than any lecture could.

Systems thinking beats quick fixes

Multi-container deployments and DevOps labs showed me a working server isn't the goal — a secure, documented, reproducible one is. That mindset now shapes how I approach any technical problem.

Good enterprise starts with the community, not the idea

Working through a Responsible Enterprise case on the Banyamulenge pastoralist community reframed how I think about "solutions" — the best ones are designed with people, not just for them.

Leadership is a daily habit, not a title

Leadership studies pushed me to see leadership as showing up prepared and taking ownership before being asked — a habit I try to carry into every project.
